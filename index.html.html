What are Global Health Initiatives?

Global health initiatives are organized efforts by international organizations like the WHO, governments, and NGOs to tackle health issues that transcend national borders. These programs focus on fighting infectious diseases, improving maternal health, and ensuring access to basic needs like clean water and nutrition.

Global Health Initiatives are crucial in achieving our sustainable development goals. By supporting these initiatives, we help build resilient communities and ensure that every person, regardless of where they live, has the chance to live a healthy life.

Local Action Plan

How we translate global strategies into our community.

Inspired by the Gavi Alliance model of immunization, our "PHSI Health Watch" project adapts international tracking methods to monitor local student vaccination schedules and hygiene practices.

Our primary goal is to establish a sustainable student-led health committee. We aim to conduct bi-weekly hygiene workshops and maintain a "clean water station" map for our local campus to ensure every student has safe drinking access.

By implementing this local plan, we expect a 30% reduction in health-related school absences and a significantly higher level of health literacy among our fellow students and their families.
